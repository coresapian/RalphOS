Upcoming Electric Vehicles: The Electric Cars You Will Soon Have Access to Purchase

By shawn On 18/03/2024 at 14:23 0

In Electric Cars

These electric vehicles are not available for purchase at the moment, but they are at different stages ranging from concept to production. It is possible that a few of them may never be released.

Electric Cars Coming Soon

Electric vehicles are the way forward, with automakers continuously expanding their EV offerings each year. Both traditional car manufacturers and newer players like Lucid, Canoo, and Rivian are investing in electric vehicle technology. Our list includes all upcoming electric vehicles, from concept to production, that will soon be hitting the market.

The anticipated release year for the Audi A6 e-tron is 2024

The Audi A6 e-tron is currently a concept, however, Audi has stated that it closely resembles what the final production car will look like.

It is constructed on the adaptable Premium Platform Electric (PPE) architecture, which allows for customization in terms of length, height, and width to accommodate various EV models.

This model will be available for purchase alongside the traditional gas-powered A6 and will be part of Audi's lineup alongside the PPE-based Q6 e-tron crossover.

The A6 e-tron concept is equipped with two electric motors that deliver a total output of 469 horsepower. All PPE vehicles come with 800-volt charging capability, and this large sedan could potentially offer a range of up to 400 miles on a single charge.

The anticipated release year for the Audi Q6 e-tron is 2024

Audi is set to expand its range of electric SUVs with the introduction of the Q6 e-tron. Positioned between the Q4 and Q8 e-tron crossovers, this new model will be built on the same PPE architecture as the upcoming Porsche Macan EV.

With a battery capacity of approximately 93.0-kWh, the Q6 e-tron is expected to offer a range of up to 315 miles.

Furthermore, its 800-volt setup will enable rapid recharging. Equipped with dual motors and all-wheel drive, the Q6 e-tron delivers 375 horsepower, while the sportier SQ6 e-tron variant boasts an impressive 482 horsepower.

Additionally, a boost mode can provide an extra 20 horsepower for each model. During a test drive of a prototype, we experienced a comfortable and well-controlled ride, along with responsive acceleration. Production of the Q6 e-tron is scheduled to commence next year, and by 2025, it will join the gas-powered Q5 in Audi's lineup.

Anticipated for 2025 are five Bentley models

Bentley intends to make a significant shift from gas-guzzling vehicles with 15 miles per gallon to brand new models equipped with fully-electric powertrains. By 2030, Bentley plans to revamp its lineup by replacing the current Bentayga, Flying Spur, Continental GT coupe and convertible with new models, with the first replacement expected to debut around 2025.

The BMW i5 M is anticipated to be released in 2026

The upcoming BMW i5 M will feature fender flares, a more aggressive stance, and 1136 horsepower. This high-performance vehicle is expected to be released in 2026. The new four-motor drivetrain will include wheel-by-wheel torque vectoring and brake-by-motor energy regeneration, allowing for piloted drifting and tank turns.

The i5 M will have a drive control unit internally known as the "Hand of God," setting it apart from the more subdued i5 model that has already been unveiled. Get ready to take the wheel of this powerful machine.

The Cadillac Celestiq is anticipated to be released in early 2024

Cadillac aims to reclaim its title as the Standard of the World, a reputation it once held without any doubt. The upcoming Celestiq, an extravagant electric vehicle (EV) that will be meticulously crafted by hand and priced at over $300K, is part of this ambitious plan.

While this price range may exclude all but the wealthiest individuals, Cadillac perceives the Celestiq as an object of aspiration rather than attainability. For the fortunate one percent who can afford these avant-garde 600-hp electric hatchbacks, each vehicle can be uniquely customized to cater to their personal preferences.

The question remains: will this be sufficient to position GM's luxury brand alongside esteemed names like Bentley and Rolls-Royce? Only time will tell, as the Celestiq is expected to grace the streets of America's most affluent communities, potentially in early 2024.

Cadillac Escalade iQ is set for the summer of 2024

The Cadillac Escalade, a large and bold SUV that has gained popularity among musicians such as Jay-Z and 50 Cent, is set to undergo an electric transformation with the introduction of the new Escalade iQ.

Featuring a powerful Ultium battery with over 200-kWh capacity, which is also used in the GMC Hummer EV, this vehicle is estimated to have a range of 450 miles. Additionally, the dual-motor powertrain can deliver up to 750 horsepower.

The interior of this luxurious vehicle boasts a 55-inch screen that spans the entire dashboard, along with a 40-speaker sound system. The Escalade iQ will come equipped with Super Cruise, General Motors' hands-free driving technology, and is expected to have a starting price of around $130,000.

Canoo pickup truck 2024

Canoo, a California-based electric automotive startup, has unveiled its latest offering in the form of a pickup truck. Currently known as the Pickup Truck, this vehicle boasts impressive specifications, including over 500 horsepower and a minimum range of 200 miles.

Despite being smaller than most mid-size pickups, with a length two feet shorter than the Ford Ranger, Canoo ensures that its pickup will provide ample space with a six- to eight-foot pickup bed.

Preorders for the Canoo pickup are now available, and production is scheduled to commence in 2024. Notably, the U.S. Army is also conducting tests on a modified version of this Pickup Truck.

The Chevrolet Corvette EV and SUV are anticipated to be released in 2025

The Corvette cinematic universe is on the verge of entering an exhilarating phase that may even surpass Marvel's standards.

Chevrolet has officially announced the imminent arrival of a fully electric Corvette, and it seems to be arriving sooner than anticipated. Despite the recent unveiling of the E-Ray hybrid, the Corvette EV will take precedence.

Utilizing the same Ultium battery platform as other new GM EVs, and considering the General's ability to propel a colossal Hummer EV weighing fifty-trillion tons to 60 mph in under 4 seconds, our expectations for the Corvette EV are sky-high.

The release date for the Dodge Charger Daytona is anticipated to be in the latter part of 2024

Dodge is committed to ensuring the muscle car remains relevant even in the era of electric vehicles, as demonstrated by the introduction of the 2024 Charger Daytona.

This new Charger model, available in both two-door and four-door variants, is equipped with two dual-motor, all-wheel-drive powertrains. The R/T version delivers an impressive 496 horsepower, while the Scat Pack variant boasts a maximum of 670 horsepower, allowing it to accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in just 3.3 seconds. With a 400-volt architecture, the Charger Daytona offers quick recharging times of approximately 28 minutes to reach 20 to 80 percent capacity.

Additionally, the vehicle is equipped with a 93.9-kWh battery, providing a range of 317 miles for the R/T and 260 miles for the Scat Pack. Sales of the 2024 Charger Daytona are set to commence at the end of 2024.

Deus Vayanne coming in 2025

Italdesign has skillfully crafted the sleek and curvaceous bodywork, while Williams Advanced Engineering has taken on the responsibility of developing the powerful 2200-hp powertrain.

According to the company, the Vayanne boasts an impressive 1475 pound-feet of torque, allowing it to accelerate from 0 to 62 mph in just 1.99 seconds and reach a top speed of 248 miles per hour. However, we remain sceptical until we witness it first hand. With production limited to only 99 units, Deus plans to commence deliveries in 2025.
